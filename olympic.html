Host City

The 2024 Summer Olympics will be held in Paris, France, marking the city's third time hosting the Games, following previous events in 1900 and 1924.

Dates

The Games are scheduled to run from July 26 to August 11, 2024, encompassing a two-week period filled with various sports events.

Venues

Events will take place across Paris and its surrounding areas, with key venues including Stade de France for the opening and closing ceremonies and athletics, Paris La DÃ©fense Arena for indoor sports, and the Grand Palais for fencing and Taekwondo. The Seine River will host swimming and rowing events.

Sports

The 2024 Olympics will feature a broad spectrum of sports, including traditional events like athletics, swimming, and gymnastics, as well as new and emerging sports.

Sustainability and Legacy

The Paris 2024 organizers are focusing on sustainability, aiming to minimize environmental impact by using existing venues and promoting green practices. The Games are also intended to leave a lasting positive legacy for the city.

Cultural and Economic Impact

The Olympics are expected to have a substantial cultural and economic impact on Paris, highlighting the city's history and culture, boosting tourism, and generating economic benefits.

Legacy Projects

The 2024 Games will promote grassroots sports, improve infrastructure, and foster international cooperation, aiming to deliver a lasting and positive impact on the city and its communities.
