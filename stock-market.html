What is the Stock Market?

The stock market is a financial marketplace where securities such as stocks, bonds, and other financial instruments are bought and sold. It provides a platform for companies to raise capital by offering shares to the public and allows investors to buy and sell ownership in these companies.

Stocks and Shares

Stocks represent ownership in a company, entitling shareholders to a portion of the company’s assets and earnings. Shares are individual units of stock, so owning shares means having a claim on part of the company's value.

Stock Exchanges

Stock exchanges are venues where securities transactions are facilitated. Major exchanges like the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and Nasdaq provide a regulated environment for buying and selling stocks. Many transactions today occur electronically through sophisticated trading systems.

Initial Public Offering (IPO)

An Initial Public Offering (IPO) is the process through which a company first sells its shares to the public. This allows the company to raise capital from investors and provides an opportunity for the public to invest in the company’s growth.

Trading

Trading involves the buying and selling of stocks based on market conditions and investor strategies. Orders can be market orders, which execute immediately at the best available price, or limit orders, which execute only when the stock reaches a specified price.
